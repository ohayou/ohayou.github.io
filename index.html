Hong Kong Dim Sim Kitchen was founded by Ho Kok Ching in 1982 as a small store on Sussex Street, Chinatown. Having worked with Dim Sim "Sifu"s since the age of 14, he immigrated to Australia with the goal to bring the original flavours of Yum Cha Finger food to the Australian public. Through the word-of-mouth of satisfied customers, and our commitment to maintaining the integrity of our products, we have gradually expanded the reach of our three operating arms; Retail, Wholesale and Catering. Our production facility is HACCP accredited and we use only 100% Australian meat, poultry and produce. From the making of the dough, to the kneading of pastry, dicing and preparation of ingredients and finally the packaging for delivery, we take great care and pleasure in each stage of the production process to ensure that each experience with our products is a memorable one.

Our Difference

Recipe

All our recipes come from the hands of Mr Ho Kok Ching, and his lifetime of eating and making his own "Dim Sum". True "Dim Sum' chefs are a dying breed. Call it old-fashioned, but for much of our 30 years of operation, we focused purely on making the best Dim Sims, Steamed Buns and Dumplings that we could everyday, and believed that our products would sell themselves. In a business where every bite counts, we continue to be meticulous about the little things, like making our bun dough fresh every morning using the same recipe, and recognising that food texture is just as, if not more important than appearance and taste. Having said this, we realise that a recipe is never perfect, and with the ever-changing needs of the Australian public, we welcome all kinds of feedback, good or bad, and are open to changes if it will better serve the customer.

Value

We make our products to be restaurant quality at the minimum, but price them so they can be enjoyed by anyone at anytime.

Ingredients

An authentic recipe is nothing without the right ingredients. The BBQ Pork used for our BBQ Pork Bun, comes as raw pork shoulder, which we slice, roast, marinade and dice ourselves, using our own custom-built furnace. We will never use any meat in any of our products that you wouldn't use for yourself at home.

Service

Complacency and inefficiency have never been and will never used to describe our company. Our foundation was built supplying the takeaway shops, mum 'n' dad restaurants and suburban haunts, with our most loyal customers having been with us for over 10 years. The late night phone calls for next-day orders, after-work deliveries, and custom orders are what made Hong Kong Dim Sim Kitchen into what it is today and is part of our DNA. If it can be done, it will be done.

Products

Pork Dim Sim

Parcels of 100% Australian lean pork mince, cabbage and onions, seasoned with spices and a hint of complimentary sauces. This is partially wrapped in a super thin pastry made fresh every morning.

Chicken Dim Sim

Minced chicken drumstick fillet, shitake mushrooms, cabbage and onions, lathered in a ginger soy sauce, and partially wrapped in a super thin pastry made fresh every morning.

Beef Dim Sim

Lean beef mince, onions and cabbage, lightly seasoned and partially wrapped in a super thin pastry made fresh every morning.

Siu Mai

Our family recipe of a classic Yum Cha staple. Finely diced pork, prawns and shitake mushrooms lightly seasoned with traditional condiments. It is wrapped in a traditional dumpling pastry made fresh every morning.

BBQ Pork Bun

Our bread and butter. An original recipe of leavened dough prepared in advance, and creamed vigorously each morning to manifest a light and airy bun that is slightly sweet, and produces an unmistakable aroma coming out of the steamer. The filling is comprised of diced bits of our very own roasted pork basted in a sticky Char Siu marinade, and enhanced with an onion-infused sauce.

Chicken Bun

Chunks of chicken drumstick fillets, shitake mushrooms, cabbage and onions, lightly seasoned and dressed in a ginger soy sauce. The bun is an original recipe of leavened dough prepared in advance, and creamed vigorously each morning to manifest a light and fluffy texture, is slightly sweet, and produces an unmistakable aroma coming out of the steamer.

Lotus Seed Bun

Boiled lotus seeds, grounded and creamed into a sweetened paste, producing a smooth, silky texture and a very light chestnut-like taste. The bun is an original recipe of leavened dough prepared in advance, and creamed vigorously each morning so that each bite is pillowy, and oozes lotus seed paste.

Red Bean Bun

Red Beans (azuki beans) that are boiled and mashed into a sweetened paste. The texture is light and creamy with an intoxicating fragrance. The bun is an original recipe of leavened dough prepared in advance, and creamed vigorously each morning so that each bite is pillowy, and oozes red bean paste.

Custard Bun

Homemade custard consisting of eggs and vanilla bean extract, whisked until smooth, and steamed into a rich, melt-in-your mouth texture. The bun is an original recipe of leavened dough prepared in advance, and creamed vigorously each morning so that each bite is pillowy, and oozes custard.

Prawn Gow Gee

A traditional Yum Cha Dumpling. Whole prawns with slivers of bamboo shoots, and a hint of Shao Xing rice wine. This is encased in a thin, translucent dumpling pastry that is made fresh each morning.

Pork and Chive Gyoza/Dumpling

Lean pork mince sprinkled with chives, onions and mashed garlic, wrapped in a super thin dumpling pastry made fresh each morning. Juicy and bursting with flavour. There is a broth inside each dumpling.

Chicken and Mushroom Gyoza/Dumpling

Minched chicken drumstick filllets, shitake mushrooms and cabbage, lightly seasoned and wrapped in a super thin dumpling pastry made fresh each morning.

Vegetable Gyoza/Dumpling

Three different kinds of mushrooms, parsley, carrots and mixed vegetables, fused together with sesame rice and wrapped in a super thin dumpling pastry made fresh each morning.
